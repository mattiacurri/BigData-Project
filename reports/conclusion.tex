\chapter{Conclusion}

This chapter summarizes the findings of the project, evaluates the achievement of the business and data mining goals, and outlines limitations and future work directions.

\section{Summary of Results}

This project developed a temporal link prediction system for social networks using EvolveGCN, in order to generate synthetic network structures starting from synthetic users.

\subsection*{Link Prediction Performance}

Two training pipelines were implemented and evaluated: the \textcolor{drawred}{\textbf{Original (sequence-based)}} and \textcolor{drawblue}{\textbf{Incremental (phase-based)}} approaches. The key findings are:

\begin{itemize}
	\item The \textcolor{drawred}{\textbf{Original implementation}} achieved superior ranking and classification performance, with MAP of 0.87 and Macro F1 of 0.86 on the best configuration (LR=0.001, NM=1).
	\item The \textcolor{drawblue}{\textbf{Incremental implementation}} achieved better discrimination ability with AUC of 0.80, suggesting its potential for threshold-independent ranking tasks.
	\item Negative sampling multiplier significantly impacts model behavior: higher values (NM=2) improve AUC but reduce MAP, indicating a trade-off between decision boundary learning and ranking quality.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Synthetic Network Generation}

The application to synthetic data revealed significant challenges:

\begin{itemize}
	\item The \textcolor{drawred}{\textbf{Original implementation}} fails to predict edges between synthetic users, attributing to the extreme dimensional collapse (PC1 explaining 95-100\% variance) that prevents the model from learning discriminative features for the homogeneous synthetic user cluster.
	\item Only the \textcolor{drawblue}{\textbf{Incremental approach}} with richer embedding space (PC1=76\%) attempts to connect synthetic users, though with poor results.
	\item The generated synthetic network exhibits unrealistic properties: average degree higher than real networks, near-zero modularity indicating absence of community structure, and extremely low average path length due to over-dense connections.
\end{itemize}

In general, while the model can learn to predict links within the real network, it fails to generalize to synthetic users due to embedding distribution mismatch: the synthetic BERT embeddings are too homogeneous (within-group cosine similarity of 0.988) compared to real users (0.872), leading to an isolated cluster in the embedding space that the model cannot meaningfully connect to the real network structure. When links between synthetic users are captured, it results in an unrealistic explosion of connections among synthetic users, further distorting the network structure.

\section{Achievement of Success Criteria}

The business success criteria defined in Section~\ref{sec:business_success} are evaluated as follows:

\begin{enumerate}
	\item \textbf{Development of a temporal link prediction model based on EvolveGCN}: \textcolor{drawgreen}{\textbf{Achieved}}. The EvolveGCN-H architecture was successfully implemented with both training pipelines.

	\item \textbf{Quantitative comparison using standard metrics}: \textcolor{drawgreen}{\textbf{Achieved}}. Comprehensive evaluation with MAP, AUC, Precision, Recall, and F1-score was conducted across multiple configurations.

	\item \textbf{Synthetic network generation}: \textcolor{drawred}{\textbf{Failed}}. The model generates predictions on synthetic data, but the resulting networks lack structural realism.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Future Work}

Based on the findings, several directions for future research are recommended:

\begin{enumerate}
	\item \textbf{Synthetic Data Improvement}: Generate synthetic posts with more diverse prompts or apply embedding perturbation techniques to increase variance and reduce the isolation of synthetic users.

	\item \textbf{Test other models}: Explore alternative temporal graph models that may better capture dynamic patterns.
	
	\item \textbf{Explore new metrics}: Investigate the usage of loss functions that account specifically for the task of synthetic user link prediction, to penalize embedding isolation or encourage diversity in synthetic user representations.
\end{enumerate}
